A Glimpse of The Caravan Housing Community

I was born and raised in Karachi, a city that I have seen change drastically in the last decade. A vibrant metropolitan hub was being slowly dragged to urban sprawl. With the fast-paced urbanization, the city became dominated by vertical buildings and overpopulation. The army launched the Karavaan housing to tackle this issue. Being a single mother, it was difficult for me to adjust to the rapidly changing landscape of the city with the advent of the caravan housing community. However, with time, we all realized that is the greater good for the city. Karavaan housing community is an innovative compact housing scheme promoting eco-friendly living. At first, it was so confusing for me to adapt to the new way of living.
The idea of KHS is to set up caravan houses in different gated areas which would have their own President and a team that will be directly in contact with the ministries of the subdivisions. Currently, the Caravans are set up on the East side of Gulshan-e-Iqbal. These caravan houses are meant to solve the problem of housing for the residents, as they are small spaces. Though these caravan houses will increase the density of people living in a specific area, they are eco-friendly and take up less space. These houses cause less carbon dioxide emissions and reduce costs, as they require low maintenance. One caravan house is good enough for giving space to a family of 3-5 people. Inside the caravan house, there is a shared space with a sofa-cum-bed and sliding beds. The storage space is within the sofa-cum-beds. We all were used to having our own separate bedrooms. It did impact our privacy a lot, however, this is the new way of life. Furthermore, having these caravans enabled more people to live in a small area than before. It has made the community quite crowded, and we feel that our privacy is compromised.

Karachi Under Crises

Heatwaves have now become more recurrent and severe. It gets unbearable to go out in the scorching heat in the summer months. Considering the whopping increase in the fatalities due to heat strokes, heat waves have now become a national emergency. For this purpose, the government of Pakistan has introduced a HeatWave Management portal as a part of their awareness campaign to prevent people from heat strokes. Moreover, the government has launched a HeatBot to regulate the indoor temperature of apartments and conserve energy. All the old air conditioners have become defunct because Karachi is dealing with electricity outages. Now people have to get the government-verified air conditioners which are only operated by the HeatBot. This HeatBot is operated directly by K. Electric. It can only work when the indoor temperature reaches above 30 degrees Celsius. They have also offered subsidized sunscreen packages because going out without applying SPF is risky.
Life has become different with the increased government surveillance. However, it is necessary as well in order to conserve and use energy sufficient energy. The technology of HeatBot is commendable. It can detect your body temperature and dehydration so you can take preventative measures against heat strokes. The bot is synced with my calendar, and it informs me beforehand of the possible chances of getting a heatstroke at the places I intend to visit during the day. While the government has come up with an excellent way for heatwave awareness, however, it is still difficult to adapt to the new lifestyle and lack of autonomy. I mostly stay at home because both my work and studies are online. Due to frequent heat waves, people tend to stay indoors, so social interactions have dwindled as well. Reminds of the time of the lockdowns during COVID-19!
