About Us

In Spring 2022, an interdisciplinary team from Habib University designed artifacts for the future as a part of a studio-based course centered on Speculative and Critical Design. The Speculative and Critical Design methodology entails how design can be incorporated to ignite meaningful reflection rather than promote blind consumption. One of the main targets of Speculative and Critical Design is to address society’s most pressing issues. As students of this course, we employed critical design as a catalyst for imagining artifacts from the future, focused on Karachi. By creating such artifacts, we developed future scenarios and explored the interpretation of our artifacts in the daily lives of people. The theme revolved around Karachi’s current environmental issues this term, and we used design research to imagine Karachi in 2050. This course aimed to envision a future that is not only surviving but also thriving despite the environmental crisis. We kept in mind the current trends and issues and designed artifacts for the future.

As a part of the transmedia storytelling project, we have built this website as a medium to showcase all the artifacts made during the entirety of the course. This website aims to offer an immersive experience of the future to our audience as well as incite a discussion through their feedback. We are thankful to our peers for allowing us to feature their artifacts on our website and to Professor Hira Zuberi for her valuable mentorship and guidance throughout this project.
